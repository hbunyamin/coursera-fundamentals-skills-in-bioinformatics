t-test

Aim

Statistical test to compare the means of two populations.

Assumptions

  • The values from each group follow a normal distribution.
  • Homogeneous, or equal, variance exists when the standard deviations of samples are approximately equal.
  • Scale of measurement applied to the data collected follows a continuous or ordinal scale.
  • Data is collected from a representative, randomly selected portion of the total population.

Analyze assumptions

Before we can employ a t-Test, we have to assess whether the fundamental assumptions hold.

Asumption 1: each group follows a normal distribution.

A histogram provides a useful visualization. Lets look at LDL level histogram:

Asumption 2: each group has the same variance.

The Bartlett test can be used to determine if the variance is similar between two samples.

ldl_vs_planet.varTest <- bartlett.test(LDL_levels ~ Planet, data = ds3)
ldl_vs_planet.varTest

Asumption 3: Independence

The third assumption is independence of samples from each other. This assumption needs to be investigated in the context of the experimental design. Let us assume, individuals are coming from different families, and there is no bias towards selection (e.g.not a bias towards younger).

Running the t-test

# first without variance-equality option
ldl.vs.planet.ttest <-  t.test(LDL_levels ~ Planet, data=ds3)
ldl.vs.planet.ttest
## 
##  Welch Two Sample t-test
## 
## data:  LDL_levels by Planet
## t = 15.044, df = 1486.1, p-value < 2.2e-16
## alternative hypothesis: true difference in means between group Earth and group Venus is not equal to 0
## 95 percent confidence interval:
##  17.12100 22.25508
## sample estimates:
## mean in group Earth mean in group Venus 
##           118.03200            98.34396
# the actual p-value
signif(ldl.vs.planet.ttest$p.value, digits=3)
## [1] 1.02e-47
# second with variance-equality option, since we tested that
ldl.vs.planet.ttest.2 <-  t.test(LDL_levels ~ Planet, data=ds3, var.equal = TRUE)
ldl.vs.planet.ttest.2
## 
##  Two Sample t-test
## 
## data:  LDL_levels by Planet
## t = 15.049, df = 1498, p-value < 2.2e-16
## alternative hypothesis: true difference in means between group Earth and group Venus is not equal to 0
## 95 percent confidence interval:
##  17.12173 22.25435
## sample estimates:
## mean in group Earth mean in group Venus 
##           118.03200            98.34396
# the actual p-value
signif(ldl.vs.planet.ttest.2$p.value, digits=3)
## [1] 9.09e-48

As a result, considering that we set a theshold for significance at 0.01, we can reject the null hypothesis, and we will consider the two populations to have a different mean.
